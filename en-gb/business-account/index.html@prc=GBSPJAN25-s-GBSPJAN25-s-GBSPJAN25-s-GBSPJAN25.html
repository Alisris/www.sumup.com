Business Account FAQs

Do I need a business account?

It depends. If you’re a limited company, you’re required by law to open a business account. This is because your business is seen as a separate legal entity.

If you’re a sole trader or freelancer, opening a business account is not mandatory. That said, some banks may prohibit you from using your personal bank account for business transactions. With that in mind, it’s generally a good idea for sole traders and freelancers to open a business account.

What do I need to open a business account?

Before we can open your business account, we first need to verify your identity. All you’ll need to do is upload a photo of an ID document, such as a valid driving licence or passport.

We may also ask you to provide a utility bill or bank statement too, as a means of verifying your address.

How fast can I open a business account?

It usually takes less than 10 minutes to open a business account with SumUp. Once you’ve signed up and verified your identity, your account number and sort code are ready to use right away, so you can instantly add money and make transfers while your Mastercard is on its way.

On rare occasions, it might take us a little longer to verify your documents, especially if the photo of your ID document is blurry or if the address you entered doesn’t match the one on your proof of address.

How much does it cost to open a business account?

Opening a business account with SumUp is 100% free. We also don’t charge a monthly or annual fee, a fee to send or receive money, or a fee to set up and use Direct Debits.

Is it really a free business account?

Yes, when we say ‘free’, we mean it. It won’t cost you a penny to open your business account, nor to send and receive money or set up and use Direct Debits.

The only fees we charge are on withdrawals that exceed your monthly free withdrawal allowance and on withdrawals made abroad. In both cases, the fee is 2%.

Can I manage my expenses?

Yes, absolutely. With the free SumUp Business Account, you can easily manage your expenses and save valuable time for your business. Here’s how:

Automated categories

  • Transactions are automatically sorted into customisable expense categories

  • All essential data is pre-filled, reducing the need for manual input

Monitor spending

  • Easily track your spending with breakdowns of expenses per category

  • Keep track of VAT payments within your expenses

Scan your receipts

  • Upload photos of your receipts and attach them to each expense

  • Store your receipts digitally to keep things organised

Easy tax prep

  • Download a CSV file of your expenses for tax purposes

  • Invite your accountant to directly manage your invoices and expenses

How do I top up my account?

You can add money to your SumUp Business Account by bank transfer or standing order. Once you've topped up, you can start using your account to make payments or purchases before your card even arrives.

Here's how to find your account number and sort code:

In the app

  1. Tap “Account” on the Home screen.

  2. Tap the settings icon in the top corner.

  3. Select “Account details” to see all of your account information.

On your computer

  1. Go to your SumUp profile, and select the “Business Account” tab on the left.

  2. Here you can find all the info you need to transfer money to your account.

For more information, check out our Support Centre.

Are there any transaction limits?

To help protect your money if your card is lost or stolen, there are certain limits on the amount you can transact (see below). You can contact our Support Team if you'd like to request a change to your limits.

Card payments (in shops and online)

  • Per payment: £15,000

  • Daily limit: £15,000

Cash withdrawals

  • Per withdrawal: £500

  • Daily limit: £2,000

Outgoing transfers

  • Per transfer: £15,000

  • Daily limit: £25,000

  • Monthly limit: £75,000

Incoming transfers

There are no limits on the amount of money you can receive.

Is SumUp a bank?

No, SumUp isn't a bank. We are a leading financial services provider and an authorised electronic money institution, regulated in the UK by the Financial Conduct Authority (licence no. 900700). This enables us to issue secure e-money accounts, account numbers, sort codes and Mastercards for your small business.

We protect our customers' funds in e-money accounts that are separate from SumUp's own bank accounts, which means your money is safeguarded against any eventuality. We believe this allows us to offer a more secure, flexible alternative to business bank accounts, providing our customers better protection against fraud with less paperwork.

How do you keep my money safe?

Your funds are protected at all times.

As an authorised electronic money institution in the UK, we're regulated by the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and operate in accordance with the FCA's rules and regulations.

This means that we safeguard your funds in segregated accounts that are unattached to our own, so that whatever happens, your money is secure and available to you.

We also use 3D Secure technology to guarantee your payments are secure. Whenever you make a payment online using your SumUp Mastercard, you'll get a notification on your phone asking you to verify the transaction.
